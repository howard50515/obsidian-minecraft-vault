# Log Summary & Reference

This document serves as a central index for all wood-generating blocks in Minecraft.

## Overworld Wood
* [[oak_log]] - The standard, most common wood.
* [[spruce_log]] - Dark, common in taigas, great for bulk.
* [[birch_log]] - White bark, common in birch forests.
* [[jungle_log]] - Very tall, found in jungles.
* [[acacia_log]] - Orange-brown, found in savannas.
* [[dark_oak_log]] - Deep brown, found in roofed forests.
* [[mangrove_log]] - Grows in mangrove swamps, often underwater.
* [[cherry_log]] - Pinkish-brown, found in cherry groves.
* [[pale_oak_log]] - Desaturated grey-white, found in pale gardens.

## Nether Fungi (Fire-Resistant)
* [[crimson_stem]] - Red, found in crimson forests.
* [[warped_stem]] - Teal, found in warped forests.

## General Wood Tips
> [!tip] Harvesting Efficiency
> While logs can be broken by hand, using an [[axe]] is significantly faster. For large-scale forestry, an axe with [[efficiency]] is recommended.

> [!tip] Processing
> All logs (and stems) can be processed into [[planks]], which are the primary ingredient for [[crafting_table]], fences, and doors.

> [!danger] Fire Risk
> Overworld logs are flammable. Be cautious when using lava or fire near wood structures. Nether stems are immune to fire.
